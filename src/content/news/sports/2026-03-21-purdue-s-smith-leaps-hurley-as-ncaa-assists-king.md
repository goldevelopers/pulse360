## Purdue's Braden Smith Breaks NCAA All-Time Assists Record

Purdue University guard Braden Smith made history on Friday night by surpassing Bobby Hurley's long-standing NCAA all-time assists record during a first-round NCAA tournament game against Queens University. Smith's achievement marks a significant milestone in college basketball, as he reached a total of 1,077 assists, eclipsing Hurley's previous record of 1,076 assists set in the 1990s.

### Historic Achievement

Braden Smith's record-breaking assist came in the first half of the game, showcasing his exceptional playmaking ability and vision on the court. The moment was celebrated not only by Smith and his teammates but also by fans and basketball enthusiasts who recognize the significance of this accomplishment in NCAA history. Hurley, who is now the head coach at Duke University, held the record for over three decades, making Smith's achievement all the more remarkable.

### Smith's Journey

Smith, a junior at Purdue, has been instrumental in the team's success over the past few seasons. Known for his ability to distribute the ball and create scoring opportunities for his teammates, he has consistently demonstrated a high basketball IQ and leadership qualities. His rise to the top of the assists leaderboard is a testament to his hard work, dedication, and skill development throughout his college career.
