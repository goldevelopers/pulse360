## Iranian Mother Addresses UN on Minab School Attack

In a poignant address to the United Nations Human Rights Council, Mohaddeseh Fallahat, a grieving mother from Iran, shared her harrowing experience following the tragic attack on a school in Minab that claimed the lives of her two children. Her emotional testimony highlighted the profound impact of violence on families and the urgent need for accountability and justice.

### The Minab School Attack

The attack, which occurred in early October, has sent shockwaves throughout Iran and the international community. Reports indicate that armed assailants targeted the school during hours when students were present, resulting in numerous casualties, including fatalities and injuries among children. The incident has raised serious concerns regarding the safety of educational institutions in the region and the broader implications for children's rights.

### A Mother's Heartbreak

In her address, Fallahat expressed her unbearable grief, stating, "My heart burns with pain." Her words resonated with many, as she recounted the moments leading up to the attack and the devastating aftermath. Fallahat emphasized the innocence of her children, who were merely pursuing their education, and called for justice for all victims of the attack.

The emotional weight of her testimony underscored the personal toll of violence in Iran, particularly on families who have lost loved ones in such tragic circumstances. Fallahat's plea for justice not only aimed to honor her children's memory but also sought to raise awareness about the ongoing violence that affects countless families across the nation.

### Iran's Response

Iran's Deputy Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi also addressed the Human Rights Council, emphasizing the government's commitment to investigating the attack and holding those responsible accountable. He condemned the violence and expressed condolences to the victims' families, reaffirming Iran's dedication to protecting its children and ensuring their right to education in a safe environment.

Araghchi's remarks aimed to position the Iranian government as responsive to the tragedy, while also navigating the complex landscape of international scrutiny regarding human rights in the country. The government faces criticism for its handling of various issues, including violence against civilians and the treatment of dissent.

### International Implications

The Minab school attack has drawn attention from human rights organizations and international bodies, prompting calls for a thorough investigation and accountability for the perpetrators. The incident has reignited discussions about the safety of schools in conflict zones and the rights of children in such environments.

As the international community watches closely, the situation in Iran continues to evolve. The testimonies of individuals like Mohaddeseh Fallahat serve as a stark reminder of the human cost of violence and the urgent need for comprehensive measures to protect vulnerable populations, especially children.
