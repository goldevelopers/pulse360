## Chinese Teen Roadtrip Film ‘Whispers in May’ Wins at Copenhagen Documentary Festival CPH:DOX

Copenhagen, Denmark — Dongnan Chen’s documentary film “Whispers in May” has garnered significant acclaim at the CPH:DOX festival, winning the prestigious Dox:Award on Friday. This accolade is accompanied by a cash prize of €10,000 (approximately $11,555), recognizing the film's compelling narrative and artistic merit.

### A Glimpse into Rural Life

“Whispers in May” chronicles the journey of 14-year-old Qihuo and her two best friends as they embark on a road trip through the remote Liangshan Mountains region of China. The film offers a poignant exploration of adolescence, friendship, and the challenges faced by youth in rural areas. By capturing the beauty and complexities of their surroundings, Chen provides viewers with an intimate look at the lives of these young girls, juxtaposed against the backdrop of a changing society.

### Festival Recognition

CPH:DOX is one of the leading documentary film festivals in Europe, known for its dedication to showcasing innovative and thought-provoking works. The Dox:Award, which “Whispers in May” received, is one of the festival's highest honors, aimed at recognizing films that push the boundaries of documentary storytelling. The award not only highlights the film's artistic achievements but also underscores the importance of diverse narratives in the global cinematic landscape.

### Impact and Future Prospects

The success of “Whispers in May” at CPH:DOX is indicative of a growing interest in stories that reflect the experiences of marginalized communities. As international audiences become increasingly drawn to authentic narratives, films like Chen's serve as vital conduits for cultural exchange and understanding. The recognition at CPH:DOX may pave the way for broader distribution opportunities, allowing the film to reach a wider audience and spark conversations about the themes it addresses.

### Conclusion

Dongnan Chen's “Whispers in May” stands as a testament to the power of storytelling in film, particularly in the documentary genre. By focusing on the lives of young girls in rural China, the film not only entertains but also educates, offering insights into the complexities of youth in a rapidly changing world. As it continues to gain recognition on the international stage, “Whispers in May” is poised to make a lasting impact both within and beyond the realm of documentary cinema.
