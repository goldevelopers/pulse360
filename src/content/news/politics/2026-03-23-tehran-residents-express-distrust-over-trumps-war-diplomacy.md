## Tehran Residents Express Distrust Over Trump's War Diplomacy

In the heart of Tehran, a palpable sense of skepticism permeates discussions among residents regarding U.S. President Donald Trump's approach to foreign policy, particularly in relation to Iran. Many citizens express a deep-seated distrust of the American leader's intentions, reflecting a broader sentiment that has been shaped by years of geopolitical tension between the two nations.

### A Climate of Distrust

The phrase "If we trust him, we lose" has become a common refrain among Tehran's populace, encapsulating the fears and apprehensions that many feel towards Trump's diplomatic overtures. This sentiment is not merely a reaction to current events but is rooted in a long history of U.S.-Iran relations marked by hostility and conflict. 

Residents cite a series of events, including the withdrawal of the United States from the Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA) in 2018, as pivotal moments that have eroded trust. The re-imposition of sanctions following this withdrawal has had a profound impact on Iran's economy, leading to widespread discontent and skepticism about any potential diplomatic resolutions proposed by the Trump administration.

### Voices from the Ground

Interviews with Tehran residents reveal a complex tapestry of opinions. Many express a belief that any overtures from the U.S. government are merely strategic maneuvers rather than genuine attempts at reconciliation. "We have seen promises before that led to nothing," said one local shopkeeper, reflecting a common perspective that views U.S. diplomacy as inherently self-serving.

Others articulate concerns about the potential for military escalation. The fear of conflict looms large in the minds of many, particularly in light of recent military confrontations in the region. "We want peace, but we are wary of what the U.S. really wants," noted a university student, highlighting the desire for stability amidst uncertainty.

### The Role of Media and Misinformation

The media landscape in Iran also plays a significant role in shaping public opinion. State-controlled media often emphasizes narratives that reinforce distrust towards the U.S., portraying Trump as a volatile figure whose policies are detrimental to Iranian interests. This portrayal is compounded by the limited access to alternative viewpoints, which can further entrench existing beliefs.

### The Path Forward

As the U.S. continues to navigate its foreign policy in the Middle East, the challenge remains in addressing the deep-seated fears of the Iranian populace. Diplomacy, if it is to be effective, must go beyond mere rhetoric and engage with the historical context and lived experiences of those affected by these policies.

In conclusion, the prevailing sentiment among Tehran residents is one of caution and skepticism. The phrase "If we trust him, we lose" serves as a stark reminder of the complexities involved in international relations, particularly in a region where trust has been eroded over decades. As both nations grapple with their respective narratives, the path to meaningful dialogue remains fraught with challenges.
