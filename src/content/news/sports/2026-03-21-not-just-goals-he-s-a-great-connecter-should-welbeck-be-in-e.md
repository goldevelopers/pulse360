## Not Just Goals: The Case for Danny Welbeck's Inclusion in the England Squad

In a standout performance on Saturday, Danny Welbeck scored two goals against Liverpool, bringing his season total to 12. This impressive feat comes on the heels of his omission from Thomas Tuchel's England squad, raising questions about the decision and whether the forward should have been included.

### A Stellar Performance

Welbeck's two goals against Liverpool showcased not only his scoring ability but also his role as a crucial connector within the team. His movement off the ball and ability to link up play have been pivotal for his club this season. The match highlighted his versatility and intelligence on the pitch, qualities that extend beyond mere goal-scoring.

### The Statistics Speak

With 12 goals in the current season, Welbeck has demonstrated consistent form that is hard to ignore. His contributions have been vital for his club, and his ability to perform in high-pressure situations, such as against a formidable opponent like Liverpool, speaks volumes about his readiness for international competition. 

### The Argument for Inclusion

Critics of Tuchel's decision to exclude Welbeck from the England squad argue that the player's current form and experience make him a valuable asset. Welbeck's ability to connect with teammates and create opportunities is particularly important for a national team that often relies on cohesive team play. 

Moreover, his experience on the international stage, having previously represented England in major tournaments, could provide a stabilizing influence in the squad. His understanding of the game and ability to adapt to various playing styles further bolster the argument for his inclusion.

### The Competition

However, the decision to leave Welbeck out of the squad is not without context. The competition for places in the England team is fierce, with numerous talented forwards vying for selection. Players like Harry Kane, Raheem Sterling, and Marcus Rashford have been mainstays in the squad, and their established chemistry with the team complicates the selection process.

### Looking Ahead

As the England squad prepares for upcoming fixtures, the debate surrounding Welbeck's inclusion will likely continue. His recent performances may prompt a reconsideration of his role, especially if he maintains his current form. The national team could benefit from his unique skill set, which combines goal-scoring prowess with the ability to facilitate play.

In conclusion, while the decision to exclude Welbeck from the England squad may have been based on various factors, his recent performances suggest that he deserves a second look. As the team gears up for critical matches, the question remains: can Welbeck's contributions on and off the ball make a difference for England? Only time will tell, but his recent form certainly warrants consideration.
