The intercontinental playoffs are a unique feature of the World Cup qualification process, allowing teams from different confederations to compete for a place in the tournament. For the 2026 World Cup, which will be jointly hosted by the United States, Canada, and Mexico, six teams are vying for the final two spots through this playoff system.

The playoffs will feature teams from various regions, including Asia, Africa, and Oceania, creating a diverse and competitive environment. The intercontinental playoffs are scheduled to take place in June 2026, just prior to the start of the World Cup. The format will involve a two-legged tie, where the teams will play home and away matches to determine which team secures a place in the World Cup. The aggregate score from both matches will decide the winner, adding an element of strategy and pressure for the participating teams.
