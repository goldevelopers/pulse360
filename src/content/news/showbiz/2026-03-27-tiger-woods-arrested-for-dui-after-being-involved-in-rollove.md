## Tiger Woods Arrested for DUI Following Rollover Crash in Florida

Professional golfer Tiger Woods was arrested on Friday after being involved in a rollover crash near his residence in Jupiter, Florida. The incident has raised concerns and garnered significant media attention, given Woods' high-profile status in the sports world.

### Details of the Incident

According to Martin County Sheriff John Budensiek, Woods, aged 50, was involved in the crash but reportedly sustained no injuries. The rollover occurred in the vicinity of his home, and law enforcement officials responded promptly to the scene. The sheriff confirmed that Woods was arrested on suspicion of driving under the influence (DUI).

### Official Statements

During a press conference held later that day, Sheriff Budensiek provided additional insights into the circumstances surrounding the crash. He emphasized that Woods was cooperative with law enforcement during the investigation. However, specific details regarding the events leading up to the crash have not been disclosed, and the investigation is ongoing.

### Background on Tiger Woods

Tiger Woods is widely regarded as one of the greatest golfers in the history of the sport. His career has been marked by numerous accolades, including 15 major championships and a record-tying 82 PGA Tour victories. However, Woods has faced various personal and professional challenges in recent years, including injuries and legal issues.

In 2017, Woods was also arrested for DUI in Florida, which led to a diversion program and a plea deal. He has since made a significant comeback, returning to competitive golf and participating in several major tournaments. This latest incident raises questions about his ongoing struggles and the impact of such events on his career.
