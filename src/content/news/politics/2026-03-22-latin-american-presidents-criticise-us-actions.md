## Latin American Presidents Criticize US Actions at CELAC Summit

In a significant gathering of regional leaders, Latin American presidents convened at the Community of Latin American and Caribbean States (CELAC) Summit held in Colombia. The summit provided a platform for discussions on pressing geopolitical issues, with a particular focus on the ongoing conflict in the Middle East. Colombian President Gustavo Petro emerged as a key figure, advocating for an immediate ceasefire in the region.

### Call for Ceasefire in the Middle East

President Petro's call for a ceasefire comes amid escalating tensions and violence in the Middle East, particularly between Israel and Palestinian groups. His remarks resonated with many leaders at the summit, who expressed concern over the humanitarian impact of the conflict. Petro emphasized the need for dialogue and peaceful resolution, urging international actors, including the United States, to play a constructive role in facilitating negotiations.

### Criticism of U.S. Foreign Policy

During the summit, several Latin American leaders voiced their discontent with U.S. foreign policy, particularly its approach to Latin America and its involvement in international conflicts. Critics argue that U.S. actions often undermine regional stability and fail to consider the unique contexts of Latin American nations. The leaders highlighted a desire for greater autonomy and respect for sovereignty in international relations.

### Strengthening Regional Solidarity

The CELAC Summit served as a reminder of the importance of regional solidarity among Latin American and Caribbean nations. Leaders discussed collaborative efforts to address shared challenges, including economic inequality, climate change, and security concerns. The summit aimed to foster a unified front in advocating for the interests of the region on the global stage.

### The Role of CELAC

CELAC, established in 2010, is a regional bloc that seeks to promote political dialogue and cooperation among its member states. The organization has often positioned itself as an alternative to U.S.-led initiatives, emphasizing the importance of Latin American voices in global affairs. The summit in Colombia underscored CELAC's commitment to addressing issues that directly affect the region, while also critiquing external influences.

### Looking Ahead

As the summit concluded, the leaders reaffirmed their commitment to work together in pursuit of peace and stability in the region. The discussions on the Middle East conflict and U.S. foreign policy reflect broader concerns about the role of external powers in Latin America. The CELAC Summit not only highlighted the need for regional cooperation but also served as a platform for advocating a more equitable international order.

The outcomes of this summit may influence future diplomatic efforts and collaborations among Latin American nations, as they seek to navigate a complex global landscape while asserting their interests and values. As the region continues to grapple with various challenges, the voices of its leaders remain crucial in shaping a collective response.
