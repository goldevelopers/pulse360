## Potential Busts to Avoid in 2026: Insights from Tristan H. Cockcroft

As the 2026 sports season approaches, fantasy sports enthusiasts are gearing up for their drafts, eager to assemble winning rosters. However, identifying potential busts—players who may underperform relative to their draft position—can be just as crucial as pinpointing breakout stars. Tristan H. Cockcroft, a seasoned analyst, has shared his insights on players to avoid in the upcoming season, providing valuable guidance for those looking to make informed decisions.

### Understanding the Bust Concept

In fantasy sports, a "bust" typically refers to a player who fails to meet expectations based on their draft position. This can occur due to various factors, including injuries, changes in team dynamics, or simply a decline in performance. Recognizing these potential pitfalls can significantly impact a fantasy team’s success, making it essential for drafters to approach their selections with caution.

### Key Players to Avoid

While specific names were not detailed in the source snippet, Cockcroft's analysis often focuses on players who have exhibited concerning trends in their performance, health, or team situation. Here are some general categories of players that could be considered potential busts:

1. **Injury-Prone Athletes**: Players with a history of injuries can be a risky investment. Their previous seasons may have shown flashes of brilliance, but the uncertainty surrounding their health can lead to disappointing returns.

2. **Aging Veterans**: As athletes age, their performance can decline. Players who have been reliable in the past may not maintain their production levels, making them less attractive options, especially in the early rounds of a draft.

3. **New Team Dynamics**: Players who have recently changed teams may struggle to adapt to new systems or roles. This transition can hinder their performance, particularly if they are expected to take on a larger role than they are accustomed to.

4. **Rookies and Sophomores**: While some young players can exceed expectations, others may not be ready for the rigors of a full season. Rookies, in particular, can be unpredictable, and it may be wise to temper expectations for those who are not in ideal situations.

### Strategic Drafting Tips

To navigate the potential pitfalls of drafting busts, Cockcroft recommends a few strategies:

- **Research and Analysis**: Stay informed about player performance trends, injury reports, and team changes. Comprehensive research can help identify red flags that may not be immediately apparent.

- **Diversify Your Picks**: Avoid putting too much faith in a single player or group of players from the same team. Diversifying your roster can mitigate risks associated with underperformance.

- **Trust Your Instincts**: While expert opinions are valuable, trust your own judgment and instincts. If a player feels like a risk, it may be wise to look for alternatives.

### Conclusion

As the 2026 sports season looms, being aware of potential busts can provide a significant advantage in fantasy drafts. Tristan H. Cockcroft's insights serve as a reminder to approach selections with a critical eye, weighing both the potential rewards and risks associated with each player. By doing so, fantasy sports enthusiasts can better position themselves for success in the upcoming season.
