## Mark Zuckerberg and Jensen Huang Join Trump's New Tech Advisory Panel

In a significant move aimed at shaping the future of technology policy in the United States, President Donald Trump has announced the formation of a new advisory panel, the President's Council of Advisors on Science and Technology (PCAST). This panel will focus on providing insights and recommendations on artificial intelligence (AI) policy, a critical area of interest as technology continues to evolve rapidly.

### Key Members of the Panel

The initial lineup of PCAST includes prominent figures from the tech industry, reflecting a blend of expertise and influence. Among the first four members are:

- **Mark Zuckerberg**, CEO of Meta (formerly Facebook), known for his role in shaping social media and digital communication.
- **Larry Ellison**, Chief Technology Officer and Executive Chairman of Oracle, recognized for his contributions to database technology and cloud computing.
- **Jensen Huang**, CEO of Nvidia, a leader in graphics processing units (GPUs) and AI technology.
- **Sergey Brin**, co-founder of Google, who has been instrumental in the development of search engines and various internet technologies.

These individuals bring a wealth of experience and knowledge that will be critical as the panel addresses the complexities surrounding AI and its implications for society.

### Objectives of the Panel

The primary objective of PCAST will be to advise the President on the implications of AI technologies and to help formulate policies that promote innovation while addressing ethical concerns. As AI continues to permeate various sectors, from healthcare to finance, the need for informed policy-making has become increasingly urgent. The panel is expected to tackle issues such as data privacy, algorithmic bias, and the potential impact of AI on employment and the economy.

### Broader Context

The establishment of PCAST comes at a time when the U.S. government is grappling with the rapid advancements in technology and their societal implications. With AI being a focal point of discussion among policymakers, the expertise of the panel members is seen as essential in navigating these challenges. The involvement of industry leaders like Zuckerberg and Huang may also facilitate a more collaborative approach between the tech sector and government, fostering an environment where innovation can thrive alongside responsible governance.

### Future Developments

As the panel is set to expand to include a total of 13 members, it will be interesting to observe how the diverse perspectives of its members will influence the direction of U.S. technology policy. The inclusion of various stakeholders from academia, industry, and government will be crucial in creating a balanced approach to AI governance.

In conclusion, the formation of the President's Council of Advisors on Science and Technology marks a pivotal step in addressing the challenges posed by AI and other emerging technologies. With leaders like Zuckerberg and Huang at the helm, the panel is poised to play a significant role in shaping the future of technology policy in the United States.
