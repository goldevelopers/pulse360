## Dana Carvey Reflects on His Iconic Lorne Michaels Impression

In a recent appearance at Studio 8H, Dana Carvey, the renowned comedian and former cast member of "Saturday Night Live" (SNL), shared insights into the origins of one of his most celebrated impressions: that of Lorne Michaels, the show's creator. This nostalgic moment took place during an episode of the NBC sketch comedy show’s new companion web series, "The Rundown."

### A Journey Back to SNL

Carvey, who gained fame for his sharp comedic timing and memorable impersonations during his tenure on SNL in the late 1980s and early 1990s, took a moment to reflect on his career and the creative process behind his impersonation of Michaels. Known for his ability to capture the essence of various personalities, Carvey's portrayal of Michaels became a staple of his comedy repertoire, showcasing his talent for blending humor with keen observational skills.

### The Craft of Impersonation

During his discussion, Carvey elaborated on how he first developed the impression, emphasizing the importance of understanding the nuances of Michaels' personality and mannerisms. "Impersonation is not just about mimicking someone's voice; it's about embodying their spirit," Carvey explained. He noted that his approach involved studying Michaels' speech patterns and demeanor, allowing him to create a portrayal that resonated with audiences.

### The Impact of Lorne Michaels

Lorne Michaels, a pivotal figure in American television, has been instrumental in shaping the landscape of sketch comedy through SNL. His vision and leadership have not only influenced the show's success but have also paved the way for numerous comedians and actors who have emerged from its ranks. Carvey's impression serves as a testament to Michaels' impact on the comedy world and the lasting legacy of SNL.

### A Nostalgic Return

Carvey's visit to Studio 8H was not only a trip down memory lane but also an opportunity to connect with the current generation of comedians and writers who continue to be inspired by the show's rich history. The atmosphere in the studio, filled with the echoes of past sketches and performances, provided a fitting backdrop for Carvey's reflections.
