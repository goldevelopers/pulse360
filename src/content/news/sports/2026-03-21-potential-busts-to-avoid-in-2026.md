## Potential Busts to Avoid in 2026: Insights from Tristan H. Cockcroft

As the 2026 sports season approaches, fantasy sports enthusiasts are gearing up for another round of drafting their ideal rosters. However, with every draft comes the inherent risk of selecting players who may underperform relative to expectations. Tristan H. Cockcroft, a respected analyst in the fantasy sports community, has shared his insights on potential busts to avoid in the upcoming season. Here, we summarize his key recommendations for players to steer clear of when building your fantasy roster.

### Understanding the Bust Phenomenon

In fantasy sports, a "bust" refers to a player who is expected to perform at a high level but ultimately fails to meet those expectations, often leading to disappointment for fantasy team managers. Identifying these players before the draft can be crucial for success, as their underperformance can significantly hinder a team's overall potential.

### Key Players to Avoid

While specific names were not provided in the source snippet, Cockcroft's analysis typically includes players who have shown signs of decline, face increased competition, or have injury histories that raise red flags. Here are some general categories of players to consider avoiding:

1. **Injury-Prone Athletes**: Players with a history of injuries can be risky picks. Their inability to stay healthy can lead to missed games and reduced performance, making them unreliable options for fantasy teams.

2. **Declining Performance**: Athletes who have shown a downward trend in their statistics or performance metrics over the previous seasons may not bounce back. It is essential to analyze trends in their play to determine if they are worth the investment.

3. **Increased Competition**: Players who have lost starting positions or face stiff competition from newly acquired teammates may struggle to maintain their previous levels of production. This is particularly relevant in sports leagues where roster changes are frequent.

4. **Age-Related Decline**: As athletes age, their physical capabilities can diminish. Players who are nearing the end of their careers may not deliver the same value they once did, making them less appealing in a draft setting.

### Strategies for Drafting

To mitigate the risk of selecting potential busts, Cockcroft advises fantasy managers to conduct thorough research and analysis before making their picks. Here are some strategies to consider:

- **Review Player Statistics**: Look at recent performance data, including both traditional statistics and advanced metrics, to gauge a player's current form and potential for the upcoming season.

- **Monitor Offseason Movements**: Stay informed about trades, injuries, and other offseason developments that could impact a player's role on their team.

- **Consider Expert Opinions**: Engage with expert analyses and rankings, like those provided by Cockcroft, to gain insights into potential pitfalls in the draft.

- **Diversify Your Picks**: Avoid putting all your eggs in one basket by diversifying your roster with a mix of reliable veterans and promising newcomers.
